Cybersecurity Insights & Updates

Dive into expert articles, news, and hands-on guides to boost your cyber skills.

By Damola Awowole | April 2025

1. Email Spoofing

Attackers forge the sender address to make it look like it's from a trusted source. Always inspect email headers if something feels off.

2. Fake Login Pages

Phishers clone real login pages to steal credentials. Double-check the URL and always use two-factor authentication.

3. Urgency & Fear Tactics

Emails or SMS that demand immediate action (e.g., “Your account will be locked!”) are usually red flags.

4. Suspicious Attachments

Files with extensions like .exe, .scr, or even .zip can contain malware. Scan before opening anything unexpected.

5. Lookalike Domains

Sites like paypaI.com (with an uppercase "I") are designed to mimic legitimate ones. Always check spelling and certificates.
